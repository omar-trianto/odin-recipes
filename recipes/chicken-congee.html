Chicken Congee

Description

Chicken congee is a savory rice porridge, traditionally made by simmering rice in a large amount of chicken broth or stock until it breaks down into a creamy, soup-like consistency. It is a popular comfort food across many Asian cultures and is often served for breakfast or as a light meal. The dish can be flavored with various seasonings and topped with different garnishes like scallions, ginger, or soy sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1L chicken stock
  • 500ml water
  • 4 slices fresh young ginger
  • 1/2 cup long grain rice
  • 200g chicken tenderloin, diced into 1cm pieces
  • 1 tsp. Chinese rice wine
  • Salt and ground white pepper
  • 2 tbsp. finely sliced green onion
  • 1/2 tsp. sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp. fried shallots
  • 2 tsp. fried garlic
  • Soy sauce

Steps

  1. Bring the chicken stock, water and ginger to a boil in a large heavy based saucepan over a high heat. Add the rice and reduce the heat to medium-low. Simmer steadily for 1 hour, covered stirring occasionally to prevent the rice from catching until thickened and most of the liquid has been absorbed.
  2. Add the chicken to the congee, cover and simmer for 4-5 minutes or until the chicken is just cooked. Add the rice wine to the congee and season to taste with salt and pepper.
  3. Serve the congee in bowls, sprinkle with the green onion, drizzle with sesame oil and top with the fried shallots and garlic. Serve with soy sauce on the side.
